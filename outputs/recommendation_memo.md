# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary
This project assessed the effectiveness of a redesigned onboarding campaign through an A/B experiment comparing Control and Treatment user groups. The aim was to establish whether the new onboarding process improves Paid Conversion Rate while keeping business performance across supporting KPIs and guardrail metrics at an acceptable level.

The analysis drew on KPI evaluation, statistical hypothesis testing, and business risk assessment to arrive at a final recommendation.

---

# North Star Metric

**Paid Conversion Rate**

Paid Conversion Rate was selected as the North Star Metric because it directly captures the proportion of users who become paying subscribers. Improving this metric feeds directly into subscription growth and long-term business revenue.

Supporting KPIs included:

* Landing Page Visit Rate
* Trial Start Rate
* Onboarding Completion Rate
* Average Engagement Score
* Average Revenue per User

Guardrail metrics were tracked throughout to ensure that gains in conversion did not come at the cost of broader business quality.

---

# KPI Tree Explanation
The KPI framework was built around Paid Conversion Rate as its central objective.

The primary performance drivers were:

* User Acquisition & Activation
* User Engagement
* Revenue Performance

Supporting sub-drivers tracked customer behaviour across the onboarding journey, covering landing page visits, trial starts, onboarding completion, and engagement levels.

Guardrail metrics — including Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, Revenue Quality, and Average Days to Convert — were monitored to confirm that business performance remained stable while conversion rates improved.

---

# Experiment Result Summary
The Treatment Group outperformed the Control Group across several key KPIs.

Key observations include:

* Landing Page Visit Rate increased.
* Trial Start Rate improved.
* Onboarding Completion Rate rose.
* Paid Conversion Rate moved from **3.17%** to **6.99%**.
* Average Engagement Score improved.
* Average Days to Convert shortened, reflecting faster customer activation.

These results indicate that the redesigned onboarding experience delivers a stronger overall customer journey.

---

# Hypothesis Test Interpretation
A Two-Sample t-Test (Unequal Variances) was conducted using Paid Conversion Rate as the test metric.

**Results**

* Significance Level (α): **0.05**
* Two-Tailed P-value: **0.0011**

Since the p-value falls below the significance level, the Null Hypothesis was rejected.

The statistical evidence confirms that the improvement seen in the Treatment Group is genuine and is highly unlikely to have arisen by chance.

---

# Guardrail Analysis
Additional business metrics were reviewed before forming a rollout recommendation.

| Guardrail Metric           | Observation                               |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Refund Rate                | Increased slightly but remained at a low level |
| Support Ticket Rate        | Rose and should be kept under observation |
| Average Days to Convert    | Improved considerably                     |
| Average Engagement Score   | Increased                                 |
| Revenue per Converted User | Declined and warrants continued monitoring |

While the Support Ticket Rate increased and Revenue per Converted User fell, neither concern is significant enough to outweigh the substantial uplift in Paid Conversion Rate.

---

# Segment-Level Insight
Performance was examined across multiple customer segments, including Region, Device Type, and Plan Type.

The Control and Treatment groups displayed a balanced distribution across these segments, reducing the risk of sampling bias. No meaningful segment-level imbalance was identified that would undermine the validity of the experiment.

---

# Final Recommendation

## **Launch**

Based on the statistical findings, KPI improvements, and guardrail evaluation, the new onboarding campaign is recommended for full rollout to all users.

This recommendation is supported by:

* Statistically significant improvement in Paid Conversion Rate.
* Higher levels of customer engagement.
* Faster onboarding completion rates.
* Improved early user activation.
* Guardrail risks remaining within acceptable bounds.

---

# Risks and Limitations
The following areas should be actively monitored following deployment:

* Elevated Support Ticket Rate.
* Lower Average Revenue per Converted User.
* Long-term customer retention performance post-conversion.
* Revenue quality over an extended timeframe.

The experiment was run over a limited observation window, and ongoing monitoring will be essential to validate sustained performance.

---

# Next Steps

1. Roll out the new onboarding experience to all users.
2. Track Paid Conversion Rate closely following launch.
3. Continue monitoring Refund Rate and Support Ticket Rate.
4. Assess long-term customer retention and revenue quality.
5. Review segment-level performance on a regular basis to identify further optimisation opportunities.

---

# Conclusion
The experiment provides clear evidence that the redesigned onboarding campaign delivers a statistically significant improvement in Paid Conversion Rate while maintaining business risk at a manageable level.

Although a handful of guardrail metrics require continued attention, the overall weight of evidence strongly supports launching the new onboarding experience to all users.
